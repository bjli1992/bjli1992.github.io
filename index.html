About

I am an Assistant Professor at the Shanghai Institute for Mathematics and Interdisciplinary Sciences (SIMIS). My work sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, statistics, and biomedicine, with a focus on developing principled geometric and AI-driven methods to understand complex, high-dimensional biological data.

Before joining SIMIS, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the National University of Singapore (NUS), Department of Statistics and Data Science, working with Prof. Zhigang Yao. I completed my Ph.D. in Computational Mathematics at Zhejiang University under the supervision of Prof. Zhenyue Zhang, and received my B.S. in Mathematics from Wuhan University (Hongyi Honors Class).

Research Interests

My primary research centers on manifold fitting and manifold learning: rigorous mathematical frameworks for recovering the underlying low-dimensional structure of high-dimensional, noisy data. I develop both the theoretical foundations and practical algorithms, and apply them to large-scale biomedical problems.
