Building bridges between mathematics and medicine.

I am an Assistant Professor at the Shanghai Institute for Mathematics and Interdisciplinary Sciences (SIMIS), specializing in biomedical data science at the intersection of applied mathematics and medicine.

My research focuses on developing geometric and AI-driven frameworks — particularly manifold fitting and manifold learning — to analyze high-dimensional, noisy biomedical data such as single-cell sequencing, metabolomics, and genomics.

I have published first-author and co-first-author work in top venues including PNAS and Nature Communications, with methods recognized internationally for advancing single-cell analysis, population-scale metabolomics, and robust clustering.

My long-term goal is to bridge rigorous mathematical theory and real-world precision medicine, enabling early disease prediction and individualized intervention at scale.

Selected Publications

Representative works at the interface of manifold theory, machine learning, and biomedical data science.

PNAS 2025 Co-first Author (1st)

Manifold fitting reveals metabolomic heterogeneity and disease associations in UK Biobank populations

Applied manifold fitting to the UK Biobank cohort (~210,000 individuals), identifying high-risk subpopulations across three major metabolic functional modules and associating them with 300+ disease risks.

Metabolomics UK Biobank Manifold Fitting Population Health
Nature Communications 2025 Co-first Author (1st)

CellScope: high-performance cell atlas workflow with tree-structured representation

A two-stage manifold fitting framework for building tree-structured cell atlases. Outperforms existing methods across 36 datasets; successfully resolved immune heterogeneity in COVID-19 patients.

Single-cell Cell Atlas COVID-19 Tree Structure
PNAS 2024 Co-first Author (2nd)

Single-cell analysis via manifold fitting: A framework for RNA clustering and beyond

Introduced scAMF, a manifold-fitting-based framework surpassing leading deep learning methods on 25 cross-species datasets. Boosted mouse embryonic stem cell identification accuracy from 57% to 100%.

scRNA-seq Manifold Fitting Clustering Deep Learning
Patterns (Cell Press) 2025 Co-corresponding Author

Global understanding via local extraction for data clustering and visualization (GULE)

A universal clustering framework using "local consistency evaluation → global structure propagation." Achieves 89.6% average accuracy across 21 cross-domain datasets.
