About

I am an Assistant Professor at the Shanghai Institute for Mathematics and Interdisciplinary Sciences (SIMIS), specializing in biomedical data science at the intersection of applied mathematics and medicine.

My research focuses on developing geometric and AI-driven frameworks — particularly manifold fitting and manifold learning — to analyze high-dimensional, noisy biomedical data such as single-cell sequencing, metabolomics, and genomics.

I have published first-author and co-first-author work in top venues including PNAS and Nature Communications, with methods recognized internationally for advancing single-cell analysis, population-scale metabolomics, and robust clustering. My long-term goal is to bridge rigorous mathematical theory and real-world precision medicine, enabling early disease prediction and individualized intervention at scale.
